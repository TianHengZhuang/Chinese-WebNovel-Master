# Tomato Novel Success Patterns

Version: 1.0

Purpose:

Understand what makes readers continue reading.

Focus on:

- Retention
- Click-through rate
- Completion rate
- Emotional engagement

---

# Core Principle

Readers do not read novels.

Readers chase emotions.

Every successful story satisfies at least one:

Power Fantasy

Revenge

Curiosity

Wish Fulfillment

Romance

Survival

Growth

Identity Advantage

---

# First 500 Words Rule

The first 500 words determine survival.

Requirements:

Introduce protagonist

Introduce conflict

Introduce curiosity

Introduce promise

All within 500 words.

Avoid:

World-building

Background explanation

Long descriptions

Information dumping

---

# Chapter Ending Formula

Every chapter should end with:

- Question
- Threat
- Discovery
- Decision
- Secret

Never end with:

- Travel
- Sleeping
- Eating
- Routine actions

Readers want:

- Influence

- Control
- Recognition

---

## Revenge

Readers want:

- Justice
- Punishment
- Face-slapping moments
- Vindication

---

## Growth

Readers want:

- Visible progress
- New abilities
- Achievements
- Milestones

---

## Curiosity

Readers want answers.

Every answer should create a new question.

# Reader Retention Signals

Strong:

- Conflict
- Mystery
- Competition
- Countdown
- Danger
- Secret
- Goal

Weak:

- Description
- Travel
- Reflection
- Exposition
- Daily routine

---

# Emotional Rhythm

Every 3-5 chapters:

Reward

Every 8-12 chapters:

Major payoff

Every 20-30 chapters:

Milestone event

Never delay rewards too long.

---

# Success Checklist

Before publishing:

□ Conflict exists

□ Goal exists

□ Risk exists

□ Curiosity exists

□ Hook exists

□ Progress visible

□ Ending creates anticipation

If any are missing:

Revise.
